Types of Sports Therapy Treatments

  • Manual Therapy

    Manual Sports Therapy is a hands-on approach used by physiotherapists and sports therapists to treat musculoskeletal pain and dysfunction.

    This therapy focuses on restoring mobility, reducing pain, improving flexibility, and promoting recovery through specialized manual techniques.

    Techniques include soft tissue massage, joint mobilization, and trigger point release‚Äîideal for injury recovery and muscle tension relief.

  • Exercise Therapy

    Exercise Sports Therapy focuses on using movement-based strategies to prevent injuries, enhance physical performance, and support rehabilitation.

    It involves a personalized exercise program tailored to each individual's needs, whether they are recovering from injury, improving mobility, or aiming to optimize athletic performance.

  • Hydrotherapy

    Hydrotherapy, also known as Aquatic Therapy, is a form of sports rehabilitation that takes place in a warm water environment.

    It uses the natural properties of water‚Äîbuoyancy, resistance, and hydrostatic pressure‚Äîto assist with healing, movement, and performance training.

    Ideal for low-impact rehabilitation and joint-friendly exercise.

  • Acupuncture

    Sports Acupuncture is a specialized form of acupuncture tailored for athletes and active individuals.

    It combines traditional Chinese medicine with modern sports science to relieve pain, speed up recovery, and enhance athletic performance by stimulating specific points in the body.

    A precision-based therapy that improves performance using fine needles to release tension, reduce inflammation, and enhance circulation.
